Introduction

Welcome to Getting a Job in Fintech in Japan

 

From 2015 to 2019, members of the Global Career Information Meetup in Tokyo have met at various places and talked about finding a path to a global career.

This book is based on the topics and Q&A from dozens of meetups with participants who are interested in a global career, working world wide, or were just curious about how to investigate working and living abroad.

Some members were interested in switching jobs or looking for a new career by changing what they have been doing.  By sharing the information via a digital book, I hope readers can find clues about career opportunities and suggestions for a better career path. Included in these pages are examples from members who have successfully embarked on a global career and have shared their methods so that you might also find a way to achieve your goals.

About the Author

The Author has lived in Asia for over 28 years, and worked in Information Technology for 18 of those years. He has an interest in using programming to create interactive tools for learning and practice and is a believer that you learn from your mistakes. This book is an attempt to create a useful tool for readers to develop strategies and ideas for finding a job overseas, and will be improved upon in the next 12 months.

Fintech

This stands for Financial Technology

A shorthand expression for venture or small companies to use technology to improve or re-invent existing parts of the financial system.

The change in the financial ecosystem in Japan since 2000 to today in 2019, and the kinds of jobs available to non-engineers.

The pool of bilingual talent in Japan has expanded both among Japanese and non-Japanese so there are more people who can work in Japan.

Due to a shrinking pool of younger workers, there are more opportunities for bilingual foreigners and also other groups of workers that hadn’t had as much opportunity before, from female workers to older workers.  

Also, there has been an increase in continuing and adult education for Japanese workers to gain certifications to work in new fields, as well as more acceptance of mid-career hiring among companies for certain specialized roles.

Chapter 1

Do you have any contacts who work in the financial industry in Japan? Anyone that you worked with before that could use your skills? If not, or you have lost touch, think about people that your contacts might know. Talk to some people about your goal to land a job in Fintech in Japan, and see if they recommend anyone. If not, or even if you start off with a list of contacts, get ready to make new contacts and try to build a list of people you can ask about FinTech.

Using Linkedin.com is one option to find people who already work in the industry, and also identifying headhunters or staffing industry professionals who can introduce you to jobs. My first job at a financial company was vis a staffing industry and involved being a translator/editor so there are a variety of jobs available, but you just need to be recognized as a specialist with the skills that are right for the role.

Meetups are another place to meet people who have some professional experience in the Financial Technology world. Try to talk to the organizers or other participants to understand more about the field and where your skills might be useful for employers.

Chapter 2

Finding jobs you are interested in can seem easy in this day of digital job boards, and social networks. Whether it is a job board for bilingual jobs, a specialized headhunter or using a social network like Facebook or Linkedin, there are many interesting jobs out there that would be great to apply for.

Trying to match your experience with a job is difficult, as for some jobs, there can be no response after uploading your resume via a job portal.  If an employer responds that is a good sign of a professional process, but many times, applicants can be faced with silence and a lack of a “read” on their chances for a position.

However, the first step is to apply for the position so looking for a job on a variety of job boards should be the first step, as well as contacting some recruiters.

Chapter 3

Downloading a resume template from https://templates.office.com/en-us/resumes-and-cover-letters to see different styles of resumes that you can use to showcase your experience and skills.

Applying for different jobs, you might want to have different resumes for each category of job you apply for. For example, if you are applying for sales jobs, you could prepare a resume for different industries or type of sales position in order to make it clearer for the hiring manager.

Chapter 4

Job role names can change and the type of experience required can vary over the months and years, so you should try to look for jobs where your experience can be used even if the name of the job role is different.

Role

  • Large Company or Small Company
  • Salary and Compensation
  • Benefits

Does the job role match what you have been doing?  If you can show some common points, the employer could bring you in for an interview in order to test that, and learn about what kind of employee you would be.

Are you applying for a job as a specialist or you can be trained to do the job? You can rewrite your resume to show common points in your experience that apply to the job functions, and also any other points in your career that can help you do the job. In this case, preparing a cover letter explaining your reasons for wanting the job can help the employer make a decision about asking you to come in for an interview.

Chapter 5

Many jobs on internet job sites or directly through company websites will require some form to fill out, either to join the site or to apply for the job. So applying through a job portal online can be time-consuming with inputting more information into the site, so a certain amount of patience is required to get through the application process.

More and more jobs can be applied via a mobile phone so filling out the information before submitting your resume can be required as part of the workflow for different job apps.

Chapter 6

Have a lot of copies of your resume on a pc or stored in the cloud. Make sure you have the latest copy of the resume downloaded and available to apply for jobs.  This seems obvious but if you have different copies, and they are stored in different places, you want to have an easy to understand title that makes it simple to search for when you need it.

If you have read over your resume and changed something to make it more usable for a particular job or role, try to note that in the title or in the folder where you store it. The aim is to make the process of applying for jobs smoother and less frustrating.

Chapter 7

If you want to get a recruiter to evaluate your resume and advise you on open jobs, find them online and reach out to them. Find the recruiter on www.linkedin.com or any of the bilingual portals.

Here is an example from Hays, a global recruiter with an office in Japan.

 https://www.hays.co.jp/en/jobs/finance-technology-jobs/

Here is a link to a Fintech community, where the companies would have connections to recruiters. https://finolab.tokyo/members/index.html.  You might want to look for in-house recruiters at startups via the Fintech Association of Japan https://www.fintechjapan.org/members/#startup, and the link is to their startup members.

Chapter 8

Having a Linkedin account can be a required part of creating and continuing to build your social network, which can lead to future jobs. While in Japan, the details of a person’s career can be seen as a private matter, not to be shared with a wider audience, the basis of Linkedin has been sharing a person’s professional qualifications with a wider social network of contacts.

Looking at jobs on Linkedin.com can give you more information about the role, future colleagues and your potential hiring manager. Being able to see more information about a company and if you have any friends working in it, or friends of friends, can be a powerful help in the job search.

Chapter 9

Try to make an appointment with a recruiter or hiring company each week. If silence and no responses have been too common in your job search, try to find ways to reach out to people directly outside of just the job referral app or online portal site.

Also, if job applications haven’t resulted in responses, try finding contact details for companies and HR managers to approach them and pitch coming in to talk. This might work better with recruiters but it can also result in scheduling a short phone call to talk to HR or a recruiter.

Chapter 10

Once you have your phone call scheduled, or you are planning your phone call, think of the apps you want to have experience with before you start using them on a job interview or exploratory call.

The phone call can be via your mobile, so make sure you’re in a quiet place, if you are going to receive a call from a hiring manager, or making one to an international or local number.

Likely it will be a local dial-in number to a conference bridge, or the HR manager will tell you which app to download in order to connect.

Chapter 11

What content in your resume or application gets you a call back from a hiring manager in Japan? It can be language ability, experience, visa status and other factors that match to the job role that you are applying for.

Ideally, you are experimenting with different resume styles or content that emphasizes your experience and fitness for the position. For Japan, other factors can be years at each job, interest in Japanese culture and showing a knowledge of Japanese culture and society.

Chapter 12

Arranging an interview is the first step, whether it is with a recruiting company or directly with a hiring company, and it often can be  over the phone. The first interview is often to confirm points in the resume and do an initial screening of the candidates for the positions. If it is a face to face interview, in Japan, it’s important not to be late for the interview and use some Japanese just to show your interest and knowledge of social customers.

Preparing for an interview can take the form of preparing a self-introduction, practicing an explanation of your background and showing examples of your experience.

Chapter 13

Are you storing your resume in the cloud? Being able to store it in a cloud service provider means you can access it anywhere, from your phone or a PC to be able to download it, share or email. Pick a cloud provider like Office 365, Box, Dropbox or another one to store your copy of your resume. The key point is being able to use the cloud to submit your resume smoothly every time.

Chapter 14

Writing a cover letter is an option for many job applications and can be an opportunity for a job applicant. On the other hand, it is more time, and there is no guarantee that your application gets a response based on the extra time spent on a cover letter, so it is difficult to assess how much more helpful it is on average.

However, it is a great way to express your desire and interest in the position and can be a great asset to refer to if you get through to an interview.

Chapter 15

Many large multinationals use a talent community to gather a collection of potential job applicants, and to build a community of qualified people who could be interested in working for them.  

Chapter 16

There are a number of bilingual job portals in Japan, aimed for different segments of the workforce. Bilingual job portals are best for people who have raised their Japanese language ability to business level or N2. There are jobs that don’t require Japanese language, and job portals aimed at foreigners of different language abilities.

Chapter 17

Have you taken a look at your English resume and tried to customize it for a particular job? Has it been a while since you updated your resume to reflect any changes in terms or to echo the job advertisement’s language? While it can be good to be smoothly applying for jobs and sending out resumes, it helps to look at the language in your resume and always be ready to make changes to emphasize skills and experience that are most important in that job you are applying for. Also, if you have friends or family that are willing to review your resume, it’s good to get some advice on how you’ve presented your experience.

Chapter 18

Looking at new roles is a good challenge as your skills and experience can transfer to different jobs in many cases. If you have always been in customer service, your skills might also be useful in customer support or in another role that is facing to customers. Terminology for roles can change based on the industry and the collection of skills that are used, so you just have to recognize where you can show that your past experience fits well with companies’ existing opportunities. Just as business opportunities continue to evolve, so do companies’ needs for skills so connecting the dots between your capabilities and their open jobs is an important part of showing your fit for the job you are applying for.
